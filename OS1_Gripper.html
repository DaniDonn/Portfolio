OBJECTIVE:

  • Design a custom gripper for a Universal Robot to pick up porous OFFs (open fiber foams)
  • Ensure a reliable grip despite the material’s high porosity

PROCESS:

  • Vacuum Section:
    • Combined vacuum system using:
      • 1 box vacuum (black component) for strong lift
      • 2 Bernoulli vacuums for stabilizing the OFF
    • Bernoulli vacuums: use fast-moving air to generate low pressure — ideal for porous/delicate objects
    • Bernoulli vacuums positioned slightly higher than the box vacuum to:
      • Keep the OFF steady during lift
      • Prevent sticking due to over-suction from the box vacuum
    • Foam padding on the bottom of the box vacuum prevents OFF movement during pickup
    • Slots in the metal plate allow Bernoulli vacuums to be adjusted laterally
    • Spacers under Bernoulli vacuums ensure level contact for smoother pickup
    • Right-angle elbow fitting and large tube provide adequate airflow to the box vacuum
    • Proximity sensor mounted on one corner (with adjustable positions) to detect successful pickup
  • Attachment Section:
    • Connects the vacuum section to the Universal Robot
    • Four screw holes on the bottom attach to the robot
    • Four corner screws attach the top section to the vacuum section
    • Long screws used to allow space for elbow pipe and tubing
  • Design and Fabrication:
    • Designed in SolidWorks
    • Applied GD&T to all technical drawings
    • Manufactured in-house at Nuvera Fuel Cells
    • Assembled and tested after fabrication

RESULT:

  • Gripper functioned as intended, successfully picking up OFFs using the hybrid vacuum approach
  • Design was effective but difficult to assemble due to long screw alignment
  • Highlighted the importance of leveling during assembly to avoid tilt
