  • Design and build an automated frisbee-launching system as a team of four during the 2022 spring semester at RIT.
  • Understand and apply the D.M.A.D.V. process (Define, Measure, Analyze, Design, Verify).
  • Accurately launch a 5-inch diameter disk to a designated target using an electric-powered mechanism.
  • Applied the Lean Six Sigma DMADV methodology (Define, Measure, Analyze, Design, Verify) to guide all phases of the Launch Master project:
    • Define:
      • Conducted extensive background research on commercial and DIY frisbee launchers, identifying key limitations in the market.
      • Captured the Voice of the Customer (VOC) through informal interviews and user scenarios, defining critical product expectations such as mobility, terrain adaptability, variable launch styles, and user safety.
      • Created a detailed Project Charter outlining scope, goals, constraints, team responsibilities, and a project timeline.
      • Mapped out customer needs and translated them into Critical to Quality (CTQ) requirements, forming the foundation of our design criteria.
    • Measure:
      • Constructed a House of Quality (HOQ) to prioritize engineering responses to VOC and CTQ inputs. Weight, motor power, wheel durability, and launch consistency were flagged as top priorities.
      • Built a Product Specification Document (PSD) with detailed and evolving engineering specs throughout the project’s development.
      • Defined safety standards and documented industry compliance (e.g., IEEE standards for electrical components).
      • Planned sensor usage for automated disc loading, including preliminary layouts for limit switches and vision-based detection systems.
    • Analyze:
      • Generated and compared multiple design concepts using a Pugh Matrix, with a human throw used as the reference datum.
      • Analyzed trade-offs between treads and wheels, flywheels and catapults, and ramp vs. hook loading systems.
      • Performed in-depth mechanical and motor analysis to evaluate flywheel design, friction loads, and torque requirements.
      • Documented all engineering decisions and rationales to maintain traceability for future design iterations.
    • Design:
      • Developed 3D models in SolidWorks for the entire mechanism, including internal and external disc cassettes, launch roller, pickup ramp, and frame.
      • Conducted a Design Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (DFMEA) to proactively assess risks related to launch stability, loading failures, and structural weaknesses.
      • Created a comprehensive system block diagram showing relationships between mechanical and electrical subsystems.
      • Planned and implemented control logic using Arduino and Bluetooth components, with documentation of wiring and code in Appendix C.
    • Verify:
      • Built and tested a full working prototype capable of movement, frisbee storage, and semi-consistent launches.
      • Developed a formal test plan outlining test actions, responsibilities, measurement methods, and pass/fail criteria.
      • Documented limitations during testing (e.g., torque shortfalls, inconsistent tensioning, structural instability) and proposed future redesigns for performance improvement.
      • Reflected on outcomes and recommended changes in team planning, prototyping timelines, and component selection to improve future cycles.
  • This project served as the case study for my Lean Six Sigma Green Belt certification. It demonstrated my ability to apply structured DMAIC/DMADV tools in a real-world engineering context, combining mechanical design, system integration, quality planning, and performance analysis over the course of a full academic year.

  • Calculated required RPM using:
    • Assumed launch velocity of 14.1 m/s
    • Radius of the disk: 2.5 inches
    • Converted to RPM using rotational velocity formulas, arriving at 2124 RPM
  • Calculated required torque using:
    • Equation: Torque = 0.5 * Roller_Diameter * (Total_Friction + μ * W * g)
    • Estimated coefficient of static friction (acrylic-rubber): 0.6
    • Calculated three friction values based on material interactions and spring compression force (F = k * x)
    • Total friction calculated to be 11.41
  • Final required torque: 10.54 lb·in
