Jabil (Jul. 2022 - Jan. 2023)

I worked at Jabil in Elmira, NY from July 2022 to January 2023 where we provided orthopedic product instruments and implants for internal and external fixation to surgically treat fractures, deformities, and tumor diseases of long bones, shoulder, hand, pelvis, hip, condyles and foot. While working there I taught myself how to code in python and learned to program computer vision and neural networks which I utilized to train a tab detection software. I also self taught myself Excel VBA which I used to create a map which can search and highlight a specific machine and area within the Jabil plant. In addition to this, I learned how to use G-code to re-program a probe on two CNC milling machines. I designed trays through creo for operators to easily identify an inspected part, created an S.O.S. button on a raspberry pi which when pressed an email will automatically be sent out to the supervisor asking for help and a light will turn on at the area sending the signal.

Acuity Polymers (Feb. - Aug. 2021)

I worked at Acuity Polymers, located in Rochester NY, from February 2021 to August 2021 where we created specialty contact lens materials for the contact lens industries to mass produce. The formula for each contact lens is made in a lab then put into large rods and into an oven. After the rods are taken out and cut into specific shapes and sizes, also known as buttons by using a CNC lathe, they are tested and delivered to the contact lens industries. My job as a mechanical engineering co-op was to create contact lenses and buttons (contact lens templates) by using a CNC lathe, design manufacturing plans for the contact lenses, and help design an automatic system for the cast molding process.
