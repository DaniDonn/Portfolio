Work Experience

Nuvera Fuel Cells LLc

(Nov. 2023 - Jan. 2025)

At Nuvera Fuel Cells, I worked as a Manufacturing Engineer, contributing to the production of hydrogen fuel cell stacks and hydrogen fuel stack engines. My role spanned several areas, including working with the Autostacker—an automated machine that assembled and stacked fuel cells—and supporting automation initiatives. I programmed Universal Robots for pick-and-place tasks and was responsible for programming Cognex vision cameras to support precise component handling. I also created an automated ticketing system using Microsoft Power Automate to streamline issue tracking. Beyond automation, I was involved in rapid prototyping, which included designing, modeling, 3D printing, and testing custom components. Additionally, I developed operator guidelines using VKS (Visual Knowledge Share), providing step-by-step instructions to standardize key processes. Each day brought new challenges that helped me grow as an engineer.

Jabil

(Jul. 2022 - Jan. 2023)

At Jabil, I worked as a Manufacturing Engineering Co-op, contributing to the production of orthopedic instruments and implants used to treat fractures, deformities, and tumor-related bone conditions. During my time there, I taught myself Python and, with support from my mentor, implemented several automation solutions. These included an interactive plant map in Excel to locate machines, a searchable maintenance database, and an S.O.S. button system—built with a Raspberry Pi—that allowed operators to quickly alert supervisors. I also used computer vision to train a Raspberry Pi to detect whether a product had a tab. Outside of automation, I designed inspection trays in Creo to help operators identify inspected parts and performed a root cause analysis on a CNC machining issue. To resolve it, I reprogrammed the probe using G-code and added alarms to flag out-of-tolerance parts before re-machining. This experience sparked my passion for automation and gave me practical skills I still use today.

Acuity Polymers

(Feb. - Aug. 2021)

At Acuity Polymers, I worked as a Mechanical Engineering Co-op, supporting the production of specialty contact lens materials used for mass manufacturing. Each lens formula was developed in a lab, poured into cylindrical rods, cured, and cut into precise shapes—called "buttons"—using a CNC lathe. My main responsibility was operating the lathe and adjusting machine settings based on the required button type. I performed quality checks by measuring initial samples for tolerance and then inspecting every tenth button using calipers. I also created engineering drawings for all contact lens and button designs, and helped design a semi-automated cast molding system to improve production towards the end of my time ther. This role gave me my first real-world engineering experience and hands-on insight into how products are made from start to finish.
