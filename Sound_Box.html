IMAGES OF CODE:

  • Build a standalone sound remote to play sound effects during a podcast.
  • Trigger different sound effects using buttons, with corresponding LEDs lighting up during playback.
  • Keep the design battery-powered, portable, and self-contained without needing a computer.
  • Planning and Prototyping:
    • Sketched out a schematic of the circuit before building.
    • Used Elegoo MEGA 2560, DFPlayer Mini, speaker, 6 buttons, 6 LEDs, 1k resistor, 1000µF capacitor, and 4-AA battery pack.
    • Prototyped with 2 buttons and 2 LEDs to test functionality.
    • Programmed the Arduino in the Arduino IDE and tested via USB.
  • Mechanical Design:
    • Designed in SolidWorks with 4 main printed parts:
      • Main controller box (holds internal components)
      • Back cover
      • Button/LED holder plate
      • Top casing with logo and access holes
    • Included 6 custom-printed button tops.
    • Used screws and heat-set inserts for secure assembly.
  • Assembly:
    • Tested the circuit with batteries before full assembly.
    • Soldered all wiring and mounted components inside the enclosure.
    • Closed and secured the case after confirming functionality.
  • Power Management Issue:
    • DFPlayer Mini draws 200–300mA and is always on, draining batteries quickly.
    • Elegoo board also constantly draws power, further shortening lifespan.
    • Added a 1000µF capacitor to stabilize voltage and extend life.
    • Implemented button rewiring to interrupt-compatible pins so Arduino can sleep until needed.
  • Future Plans:
    • Design a custom PCB to:
      • Reduce wiring and overall size of the device
      • Improve battery efficiency and lifespan
      • Make the unit more compact and power-optimized
  • Device functions as intended — sound plays and LEDs light when buttons are pressed.
  • All electronics and 3D-printed parts were integrated successfully.
  • Battery drain is still a challenge but is being actively addressed.
  • Project is still in progress, with improvements being added over time.
