RESULT:

  • Design a custom pickhead for a Universal Robot to perform precision pick-and-place tasks involving rivets as small as 2mm in diameter.
  • Ensure the pickhead is compact, lightweight, and compatible with the Universal Robot’s quick-connect system.
  • Create a cost-effective, manufacturable design that can securely handle rivets without the need for additional gripping mechanisms.
  • Concept and Design:
    • Modeled the pickhead in SolidWorks, focusing on a two-part design to ensure modularity and ease of assembly.
    • Designed the Rivet Tip as a conical piece with a 2.1mm hole for rivet suction, threaded on top to connect to a spring-like air tube connector.
    • Created the Top Section with a hockey puck-style connector for the quick-connect system, allowing for rapid attachment and detachment to the robot arm.
    • Quick Connect Description: A quick connect is a mechanical coupling that allows for rapid attachment and removal of the pickhead from the robot arm, minimizing downtime and simplifying tool changes.
    • Incorporated a long, cylindrical section with a rectangular cutout to allow the air tube to pass through, and a threaded lower section to connect the spring connector.
  • Prototype Development:
    • Printed the design on a Markforged 3D printer using Onyx, a carbon fiber-infused nylon known for its high strength, durability, and dimensional stability.
    • Paused the print mid-way to insert drop-in metal threads for added durability and secure component attachment.
  • Testing and Optimization:
    • Conducted initial functionality tests to validate the pickhead’s gripping and mechanical performance.
  • Final Manufacturing:
    • Drafted detailed engineering drawings and sent the design to an outside manufacturer for precision machining.
    • Received the final, machined version and verified its performance on the production line.
  • Successfully developed a rivet pickhead that met the project’s functional and design requirements.
  • The pickhead is currently in active use at Nuvera, demonstrating long-term reliability and performance.
  • The design effectively handled small rivets without the need for additional gripping mechanisms, reducing overall tooling complexity.
